Nikos Malakasis

Computational neuroscientist, based in Munich.

Bio

I'm a PhD candidate at the Computation in Neural Circuits Lab at TU Munich, led by Prof. Dr. Julijana Gjorgjieva. My research focuses on how inhibitory and excitatory synaptic plasticity shape the subcellular organisation of synapses on dendrites — the branching structures through which neurons receive and integrate signals.

Beyond research, I supervise master's and bachelor's theses, teach classes, and contribute to grant writing. I hold an MSc in Bioinformatics from the University of Crete (9.4/10) and a BSc in Biology from the National and Kapodistrian University of Athens. Originally from Greece, I've lived and studied across Europe — Athens, Crete, Braga, and now Munich.

Outside of work, I'm into video games, generative AI art, graphics design, video editing, martial arts, snowboarding, strategic board games, trading card games, and anime.
